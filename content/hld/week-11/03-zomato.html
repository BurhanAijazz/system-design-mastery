Zomato / Food Delivery Platform Design

Food delivery is a three-sided marketplace: customer, restaurant, and delivery partner. Unlike Uber's two-sided model, the added complexity of food preparation time creates a unique timing coordination challenge -- the delivery partner should arrive at the restaurant exactly when the food is ready.

Define: Requirements

  • Functional: Browse restaurants (location, cuisine, rating), view menu and order, restaurant receives and prepares order, assign delivery partner with real-time tracking, payment (prepaid or cash), ratings for restaurant and delivery
  • Non-Functional: Order placed within 2 seconds, delivery partner assigned within 60 seconds, real-time tracking every 5 seconds, average delivery 30-40 minutes, 99.9% order success

Three-Sided Marketplace Challenge

# Timing coordination is the key difference from Uber:
#
# Customer places order at 12:00
# Restaurant starts cooking (est. 20 min prep)
#
# If partner arrives at 12:10 -> waits 10 min (wasted time)
# If partner arrives at 12:25 -> food gets cold (bad experience)
#
# IDEAL: partner arrives at 12:20 exactly when food is ready
#
# Solution: predict prep time per restaurant (ML model),
# dispatch partner with estimated arrival = predicted prep time

Estimate

# 50M MAU, 10M DAU, 500K restaurants, 300K delivery partners
# 5M orders/day (peak 15M on holidays)

# ORDERS:
#   5M / 86400 = 58 QPS (peak lunch/dinner ~300 QPS)

# DELIVERY PARTNER LOCATIONS:
#   300K partners x 1 update/5 sec = 60K updates/sec

# ORDER TRACKING:
#   500K concurrent active orders at peak
#   500K x 0.2 updates/sec = 100K pushes/sec

# STORAGE:
#   Orders: 5M/day x 5 KB = 25 GB/day = 9 TB/year
#   Restaurant menus: 500K x 50 items x 500B = 12.5 GB (small!)
#   Partner locations: 60K/sec x 50B x 86400 = 259 GB/day

Architecture

  • Restaurant Service: Menu management, availability, prep time estimation. Menus cached aggressively (change infrequently).
  • Order Service: Order creation, state machine (placed -> accepted -> preparing -> ready -> picked_up -> delivered).
  • Dispatch Service: Matches orders with delivery partners based on location, estimated prep time, and partner availability.
  • Location Service: Tracks 300K delivery partners, geohash-indexed for fast nearby queries.
  • Tracking Service: Real-time order status and delivery partner location pushed to customer via WebSocket.
  • Payment Service: Handles prepaid orders and cash-on-delivery reconciliation.

Order State Machine

  • Placed: Customer submitted order, sent to restaurant
  • Accepted: Restaurant confirmed, cooking begins
  • Preparing: Food being cooked, prep time countdown active
  • Ready: Food ready for pickup, partner notified
  • Picked Up: Partner collected food, en route to customer
  • Delivered: Customer received food, payment finalized

Intelligent Dispatch

  • Predict restaurant prep time using historical data (ML model per restaurant)
  • Calculate optimal dispatch time = prep_time - estimated_travel_time_to_restaurant
  • Order batching: if two orders go from nearby restaurants to nearby customers, one partner can handle both
  • Re-assignment: if assigned partner becomes unavailable, quickly find a replacement

Key Design Decisions

  • Restaurant menus are heavily cached: Small dataset that changes rarely, read millions of times per day
  • Prep time prediction is critical: Poor estimation leads to either cold food or waiting delivery partners
  • Peak handling: Lunch (12-1 PM) and dinner (7-9 PM) peaks require auto-scaling of order and dispatch services
  • Cash-on-delivery: Adds reconciliation complexity but is essential in markets where digital payments are not universal
